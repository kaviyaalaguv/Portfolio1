Overview

CalmSpace was designed to help users handle overwhelming emotions through simple, guided experiences that reduce mental load and encourage emotional recovery through small actions.

Problem

Many wellness apps overload users with features, content, and decisions. When users already feel anxious, overwhelmed, or emotionally exhausted, complex interfaces often increase friction instead of helping instantly.

Solution

The solution focused on creating a minimal and emotionally calming experience where users can quickly select how they feel and follow a guided reset journey without pressure or cognitive overload.

Entry Experience

The entry screen was intentionally designed with minimal distractions to create a calming first impression and encourage users to begin without hesitation.

Mood Selection

Users select their emotional state to receive a personalized flow that adapts the experience based on how they currently feel.

Guided Experience

The guided flow breaks emotional recovery into smaller, manageable steps that reduce pressure and encourage users to continue without feeling overwhelmed.

Focus & Completion

Soft progress indicators and calm interactions help users stay engaged while reinforcing small achievements throughout the experience.

Key UX Decisions

• Step-by-step guidance instead of overwhelming instructions
• Minimal UI to reduce mental fatigue
• No stressful countdown timers
• Emotion-based personalization flow
• Calm visual hierarchy and soft interactions

UX Thinking

The interface focused on reducing cognitive load through simplified decisions, calming visual spacing, and emotionally supportive interactions. Every screen was designed to feel lightweight, reassuring, and easy to follow during emotionally difficult moments.

What I Learned

This project helped strengthen my understanding of emotional UX, calming interaction patterns, and designing experiences that prioritize user mental state rather than feature complexity.
